You meet a fellow mathematician who decides to test you before telling you about the path.
"Have you heard about the Invariance Principle? If there is repetition, look for what does not change! For example, let \(0 < b < a\) be real numbers. Their harmonic mean is defined as \[ \frac{2ab}{a+b}, \] and it is trictly less than the usual arithmetic mean. Suppose we define then two sequences \((x_{n})_{n\in \mathbb{N}}\) and \((y_{n})_{n\in \mathbb{N}}\) by setting \(x_{0}=a\), \(y_{0}=b\), \[ x_{n+1}=\frac{x_{n}+y_{n}}{2} \text{ and }y_{n+1}=\frac{2x_{n}y_{n}}{x_{n}+y_{n}}. \] What is the limit of each sequence?"
